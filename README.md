Happy Birthday, Wanda June (WJN) — Kurt Vonnegut, 1970. A satire of toxic heroic masculinity — Odysseus's homecoming retold as a violence-loving hunter returning to a world that has moved on without him. THE THEME: The warrior comes home a hero and finds the house has changed the locks. Heaven, meanwhile, is a shuffleboard court. THE PURPLE THREAD: Self-contained — but on the Purple Thread by device: its HEAVEN scenes (the cheerful dead on a shuffleboard court) prefigure the afterlife Vonnegut would reuse in Galápagos's blue tunnel and God Bless You, Dr. Kevorkian.
